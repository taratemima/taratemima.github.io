The Gypsy Camp is Gone

They just went up and left.
All the banners that hung so bright
are now limp on the ground.
The bonfires I knew as a child
have now turned to ashes.

Where are my elders?
Where are the fortunetellers?
Where are the people who dance in the moonlight,
kicking their feet to the violin?

They all gone away,
and left me in the classroom
watching the sky.

They've taken jobs in big companies
preoccupied with the year 2000 problem.
If you remind them of who they were,
they'll just turn their backs
and pretend you said nothing.

I fall asleep
on the leafbed.
They've gone dead like autumn,
and no one will know what was lost.

I am the last one
bedazzled and partial to vows and quests.
I swore by the red leaves,
and my hot tears,
that the life I once knew
won't die before I do.
