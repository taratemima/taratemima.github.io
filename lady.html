I first saw her in an office, in the midst of clicking keys and TLAs. We talked about the Jargon File and she seemed excited that I had the html version on my bookmark file. She was small and attractive, with blond hair and a cupid mouth. Even though she was in high school, she could talk to me about books and music I know about and even tell me about things I didn't know about (which can be ego-bruising at times). I liked her, but she was dating someone, and I have a policy about such things.

We went down to Tosci's for ice cream. We talked about the joys of hazelnut ice cream. I think the conversation went something like this. I mentioned in some casual context or another that I was bisexual. "Oh well, I was wondering on whether or not to make a pass at you." I giggled into my ice cream cone over the relevation.

But still, she was dating some kid. She sent me email telling that she was dating the guy out of boredom and that he will be leaving in three weeks. In the meantime, she would rather not get into a relationship.

I respected that, so I tried to leave her alone. I wrote her a poem and begged her to think nothing of it. I written more about and felt inadequete that my inspiration didn't come from anything intirguing to other people like quantum physics or Arabic matriarchies. I dated someone who broke up with me after four days (and spawned a long tortured letting go, but that's another therapy session :)), but I still thought of her. I was terrified of what I thinking of. I mean, an old trusted friend dated her and told me that she "didn't like sex much" and had a mean streak. Eeek, I have a temper and am perputually horny; whatever relationship that comes out of it is as good as doomed. Of course, he did things like, as she said it, "felt [her] up in Downtown Crossing."

She went on to date some other kid, making me wonder what was wrong with me. Everytime I see her, I wonder what I'll say. Once she went off with a group of kids, and I restrained the urge to yell "Wait, come back!" I got scared when she didn't answer mail or respond to talk for I-can't-remember-how-long, prompting her to send me mail telling me "just chill. By the way, UMB talk (function) doesn't work."

Other than desperate email communication, my infatuation manifested in weird ways. For example, I don't like Tori Amos all that much. If I wanted mystical dreamy vocals, I'd put on Kate Bush. Yet, I am fixated on Little Earthquakes, especially the songs "Crucify" and "Girl ("She's been everybody else's girl/ maybe someday/she'll be yours"--oops, wrong lyrics, I'm hearing what I want to hear)." As I write this, it seems absurd, but then again, I haven't listened to it in a while. I also had been looking at pictures of skinny wavy-haired blondes with cupid bow lips, blue eyes and slender wrists rather intently lately.

What was a annoying yet enjoyable infatuation is now critical. I am in relationship with a very sweet guy who likes me. I should be happy; that girl should be at the back of my mind. Then I see her again at Arisia with red hair and those *lips* colored black. If this keeps up, he'll feel neglected and leave me. There is a possiblity that a real love will be dashed because of some castle-in-cloud fantasy. My dear old friend is probably sick of my obsessing over his ex-girlfriend. *Arrrrrrrghhhhh*

Doubtlessly, I will piss some people off. I will piss off the girl, and the fact that this unrequited love is over the World Wide Web. I will piss off the guy--why haven't I written anything about him? This is a last resort. Maybe I'll find strength enough to love myself in the aftermath.

Update: I broke up with the guy I was dating when I wrote this (around March of this year). I am okay, not as tortured about her; I've on to other futile unrequited romances.

Update (1999): I've forgotten her, at least I don't wonder what could have been any more. It never was.

The tendency, it's still there.

Tara Edwards, 1997
