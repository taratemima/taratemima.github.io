A. "Peach, mango, banana"
Just by the stairs in Harvard Square Station, a man makes fruit smoothies, sometimes with fruit juice bases, some with milk and honey. I like to take the seasonal fruits and mix them in different mixtures. It was there I learned to love mango. Before then, I thought it tasted like cantoloupe, which I was not enthusastic about (then again, I perfer it to papaya--yech!). I found mango complemented the other fruits well, even though I tend to stick to the fruits closest in color to it. 

When I can, I like to have a peach, mango, and banana smoothie with a milk and honey base. I like to watch the orange-yellow-pink shades mix into the colors of the early stages of a sunset.

B. "Books appear in my dreams"

I was told once books appearing in your dreams was a bad omen, but I am never sure if it is true. I just remember often reading about subjects and opinions that would not make any sense in the cold light of day, but are oddly compelling and believable in the dream state. I am guessing all this, however. When I try to read a passage again, the words become different from what I remember, or they fade from memory, no matter how much I try to remember. Maybe the only lucid thought I have in dreams is "It is written down, therefore, it must be important."

C. "I'm sorry for forgetting to pray every night, like I said."

"So, this is my prayer for a strange city, o God, o Light of all lights, from the neural spark of consciousness to the light of far away stars. Am I calling You by the right name? I wonder if I am doing the right things. Why am I lonely often, and yet only engaging in small conversations with people, finding it easier to read, eat, think, walk on my own? I know that writing is what I am best at, how I express myself, and it seems all the other skills are incidental, there to present my writing or earn me money. Am I doing my best by this gift? Or are there other gifts that I am neglecting due to perservation? Am I being too considerate of other points of view, sometimes taking them as my own, even if I am not sure if they are? Or am I too set in my own views? I apologize for not praying every night like I said--perhaps that in my own way will clear things up."

D. "The short life of 'Fast & Bulbous'"

I made a zine at sixteen because I loved music and writing, and wanted to write about music and everything else I liked. My only exposure to culture was The Noise, The Pit Report and maybe Spin, but then I expanded into small lovingly made zines about atmospheric music and Maximum Rock'n'Roll. I was small and made of newspaper clippings I collected and drawings I did. Real cut and paste work--I even used markers for some of the headings. It was free because I really wasn't sure anyone would pay for it. I did get to interview people I liked: Joe Bonni, Scott Getchell, Margaret Weigel, Janet Egan, at least two the members of Think Tree. 

It never made it pass issue 3. I suspect it had more to do with things getting in the way--I was actually having a social life, something I never thought I would have much of in high school (aside from in-school conversations with people, visits to the Pit, and the tortured unrequited love I had for some 23-year-old homeless guy who I am now in a long distance relationship with). Maybe my interests and concerns got too big for one six page zine. 

E. "I've stopped getting drunk enough to fall asleep in the middle of the room"

I thought people didn't really like me, only tolerated me, and it drove me to drink. I figured I could forget my fears and just be the chatty, witty sort that I always envied. Over time, I realized 1) I said stupid stuff while drunk, 2) I fall asleep after a certain point, and 3) people like me when I am awake and not saying anything unintentionally gross or over-sharing. So, I keep it to one drink, maybe two. Of course, considering I am abstaining from alcohol due to recovering from mono, I might get back into the habit of being stone-cold sober at parties.
