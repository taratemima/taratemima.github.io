"You can always light a candle for it," Erik suggested when I said that I may be too busy to do anything for Imbolc. I intended to do just that. I couldn't remember where I left candles, and I wasn't sure how to explain to my mother why I was lighting a candle in the daytime. I put the problem aside.

Fast forward to Imbolc, Sunday afternoon. I was getting food when I saw a lit candle on the stove. Waitasec . . . "Did you light this, Mom?" "Um-hum."

I am not sure how to explain it. Psychic suggestion? Chain of transmission of Celtic traditions? Or did she just wanted the kitchen to smell nice. I'd love to believe the former, but I am inclined to believe the later.
