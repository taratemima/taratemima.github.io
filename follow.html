Don't Follow

sometime in the future . . .

"Say goodbye don't follow"

Cartons of spareribs and fries scatter all over the table.
Me and he are watching the sun go down,
staining his hands red.

I ask so many questions, he said--
are you all right, taking your Zoloft?
how is your job, settled accounts?

Why doesn't *he* ask the questions--
do you love me (oh, god, as a friend),
are you moving yet (someday soon)?
packed your bags yet (I want to, but . . .),
thrown away my photos yet (never)?

I hope he doesn't but I wish he would.

He gets a glass of water and his bottle
it's time for his Ritalin--
I don't take mine until eight tonight.

I will miss his wolfish eyes.
He will miss my habit of blushing easily.
During dinner we were alone.
After, we will be even more alone.

"Read the page it's cold
And dead

Take me home"

The first and last lines were from Alice In Chain's "Don't Follow." The phrase came to mind, I wrote the poem, looked at the song, and took it from there.
