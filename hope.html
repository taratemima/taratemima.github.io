From a message I posted to Elbows:

 While I was on the train, two punkish girls came up to me and asked me "'Scuse me, but are you the girl from Mazzy Star?" I told them "no," but I wonder what their reaction would be if I said 'yes'? Autographs? Offerings of their bodies (I wish)?

 It was just weird. Where they got the idea that the Hope Sandoval wore glasses and rode the T, I'll never know.

 It reminded me of a story of a poster to the netgoth list. He was annoyed with "he touched me!" effusiveness that someone on the list expressed for some member of a local gothic rock band. He responded by telling of an obnoxious member of a well-known local (Boston) band and his coterie of pubescent girls. In response, I joked "Well, he's cut out for a job with Marilyn Manson!" I told him a story about a girl who got herpes from the "dreadlocked bass player" of MM.

 He emailed me back, and said, to paraphrase him, "That's exactly what I'm trying to tell her! Some people use their fame to get people to do what they want, and it's not always good for the fan."

 I suddenly was glad I told the truth and didn't fall into the exploiting trap, even for a second.
